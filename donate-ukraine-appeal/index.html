There is no safe place in Ukraine. The deaths of three children reported in the conflict in Ukraine shows that every war is a war against children.

The children of eastern Ukraine have lived in the shadow of conflict for eight years. In late February 2022, heavy weapons fire along the contact line in eastern Ukraine – a strip of land that divides government and non-government-controlled areas – has already damaged water infrastructure and schools.

Unless the fighting subsides, tens of thousands of families could be forcibly displaced, dramatically escalating humanitarian needs. The conflict has put the safety and wellbeing of millions of children at risk.

What does the conflict mean for Ukraine’s children? 

7.5 million children will be impacted by the conflict and require access to basic services.

Damaged water infrastructure is threatening access to safe drinking water for more than 4 million people.   

More than 350,000 school children currently have no access to education.
